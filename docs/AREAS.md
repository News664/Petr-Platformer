# Area themes — the seven regions

Castlevania-style structure: every region is a set of rooms with one
unified aesthetic, one unified enemy family, one themed puzzle verb, one
Sanctuary, usually one boss, and one ability unlock. A room should be
identifiable as belonging to its region from a single screenshot.

Shared vocabulary across all regions: Waystones (rescue points), motes,
anchored statues, reset-warded puzzle chambers, one hidden room minimum
per region (the map hints at it with an unlabeled outline).

---

## 0 · Petrified Village — *the home you keep returning to*

- **Role:** tutorial + permanent hub. No boss, no enemies. (Built.)
- **Aesthetic:** whitewashed cottages under stone dust, warm lanterns
  still burning against cold marble; laundry lines frozen mid-flap.
  Palette: chalk whites, slate blues, lantern amber.
- **Puzzle theme:** the basics of Soften/follow/Grace, statue-as-terrain.
- **Ability:** Soften I (start) · **Mason's Grip** at the Sanctuary
  relight — statues become pushable, which re-opens every Village room
  with a second solution layer and teaches "abilities change old rooms."
- **Rescue density:** high — this is where rescued NPCs visibly return.

## 1 · The Quarry — *stone as labor*

- **Rooms:** ~12 + boss + Sanctuary. First real region.
- **Aesthetic:** terraced cliff cuts, scaffolds, cranes, rope elevators,
  half-carved colossus blocks; dawn light through rock dust. Palette:
  ochre, rust, bone-white cut stone.
- **Enemies (first enemies of the game):** *Mortar-slimes* (glue statues
  and doors in place — the region's lock mechanism) and *Quarry wisps*
  (skittish light-eaters that drain Chisel Light on touch, teaching
  resource protection). Both genderless.
- **Puzzle theme:** **physics and weight** — pushing (fresh from Mason's
  Grip), rolling rounded blocks down ramps, crane counterweights where a
  statue on a platform lifts a cage elsewhere, statue-rolling across
  crushers.
- **Boss:** *The Foredame* — a colossal half-carved statue animated by
  the curse, fought by baiting her fist-slams onto cracked pillars.
  First boss = a statue, seeding the question of what the curse can
  puppet.
- **Ability:** **Chisel Dash** (Quarry Sanctuary) — shatter cracked
  stone; opens the Village cellar on the way back (first big backtrack
  beat: Master Ida's workshop, lore payoff).

## 2 · Sunken Baths — *stone versus water*

- **Rooms:** ~14 + boss + Sanctuary + the flooded stair link to the
  Village square.
- **Aesthetic:** marble bathhouse terraces, black still water, steam
  vents, mosaic floors, drowned lanterns glowing green under the
  surface. Palette: verdigris, ink-black water, pale marble, candle gold.
- **Enemies:** *Silt sirens* (humanoid, female — their song lures
  softened NPCs off your path, hijacking the follow behavior) and
  *Cistern crabs* (armored, immune until flipped by a dropped statue).
- **Puzzle theme:** **buoyancy and weight** — flesh floats, stone sinks,
  applied in both directions: sink statues to make underwater steps,
  self-petrify to walk lakebeds, drain/flood chambers with statue-weight
  valves.
- **Boss:** *Mother Lye* — a bloated siren choir-mistress; her arena
  slowly floods, forcing rhythm between swimming (safe from slams) and
  petrified anchoring (safe from currents).
- **Ability:** **Self-Petrification** (Baths Sanctuary) + Soften II at
  the optional deep mini-sanctuary.

## 3 · Gorgon Gardens — *being seen is being stone*

- **Rooms:** ~14 + boss + Sanctuary.
- **Aesthetic:** overgrown palace gardens, topiary walls, moonlight,
  reflecting pools and polished bronze mirrors; petrified birds mid-
  flight on wires of ivy. Palette: deep greens, moonlit silver, marble.
- **Enemies:** *Gorgon acolytes* (humanoid, female — petrifying gaze
  cones, the region's core threat) and *Basilisk hounds* (patrolling
  gaze on a timer). Being gazed = petrified in place (recoverable at
  checkpoint; self-petrify blocks it — the shield is becoming the thing
  they'd make you).
- **Puzzle theme:** **line of sight** — stealth lanes behind statues,
  mirror-angling to reflect gazes back (a reflected acolyte becomes a
  statue-tool), softened NPCs walked through gaze fields *because they
  are already immune while stone* — timing their soften windows between
  sweeps.
- **Boss:** *The Curator* — petrifies waves of her own acolytes to build
  walls; soften the right "bricks" to collapse her architecture.
- **Ability:** **Harden** (Gardens Sanctuary).

## 4 · Crystal Undercroft — *light is a resource*

- **Rooms:** ~12 + boss + Sanctuary.
- **Aesthetic:** black caverns veined with amethyst geodes (her stone —
  personal), resonant crystal organs, bioluminescent dust; the amulet is
  the main light source. Palette: near-black, violet, cyan glints.
- **Enemies:** *Crystal wasps* (sting = partial petrification: stone arm
  or stone legs — debuff-that's-also-a-buff) and *Echo shades* (only
  petrifiable while singing).
- **Puzzle theme:** **light and timing** — carrying light, crystal
  resonance switches, precision mid-air re-freezes; the region for the
  game's hardest optional puzzles.
- **Boss:** *The Unlit* — a shade that snuffs the amulet; fought by
  relighting crystal nodes with Harden/Soften chains in darkness.
- **Ability:** **Remote Soften** (Undercroft Sanctuary).

## 5 · Marble Palace — *the curse's showroom*

- **Rooms:** ~15 + boss + Sanctuary.
- **Aesthetic:** cold perfection — colonnades, mirror floors, curated
  statue galleries with brass placards (the Curator's collection:
  people, labeled like art). Palette: white, gold, blood-red drapes.
- **Enemies:** *Stone-wardens* (re-petrify your softened NPCs mid-
  escort — the anti-rescue enemy) and *Placard knights* (animated armor
  displays, humanoid).
- **Puzzle theme:** **everything at once, choreographed** — multi-statue
  Soften III escorts, warden interference, gallery puzzles where the
  "exhibit layout" must be rearranged to open doors.
- **Boss:** *The Curator, restored* — rematch with her full collection.
- **Ability:** **Soften III / Sustain** (Palace Sanctuary).

## 6 · The Heart of Stone — *the first statue*

- **Rooms:** ~8, linear gauntlet remixing one signature puzzle from each
  region. No new enemies — curse-warped finales of each family.
- **Aesthetic:** inside the calamity: a cathedral of fused statues,
  gray-on-gray, the only color Amethyst herself and the amulet's violet.
- **Boss/ending:** the gorgon — outcome per §4.5 of the GDD (bad /
  mediocre / good / true).
- **Ability:** none — the finale spends everything the player has.

## Consistency rules

1. One enemy *family* per region (2 types + boss); enemies never
   migrate between regions except curse-warped finale versions.
2. One puzzle verb dominates per region; other verbs appear only as
   seasoning after their home region.
3. Every region: 1 Sanctuary, 1 boss (Village excepted), ≥1 hidden
   room, ≥1 anchored statue set piece, 4–8 rescuable NPCs with one
   Witness among them (6 Witnesses across regions 1–5, plus the
   Village's own in the cellar).
4. Palette discipline per the style bible; a region's tileset, enemies,
   and CGs share its palette row.
